#Sala za Kikatoliki 
-

This lightweight, single-file solution offers a high-performance, seamless experience across desktop, mobile, and tablet devices without the need for an active internet connection after the initial load.

The app features a robust language engine that allows users to toggle instantly between Swahili (Kiswahili) and English. Unlike standard translation tools, the content has been curated to respect the specific liturgical phrasing used in Tanzanian and English-speaking Catholic communities.

Engineered with modern web standards, the app is fully installable. It utilizes a web manifest to appear as a native application on iOS and Android home screens, offering a full-screen experience and offline reliability.

üåì Accessibility & Personalization
Dark/Light Mode: User-selectable themes to reduce eye strain during evening vigils or morning prayers.
Adjustable Typography: Dynamic font scaling within the settings menu to ensure readability for all age groups.
User Persistence: The app greets you by name, creating a personal digital sanctuary for your prayer life.

üôè Detailed Prayer Library
The Holy Rosary: Includes automated mystery selection based on the day of the week (Joyful, Sorrowful, Glorious, Luminous).
Divine Mercy: A dedicated section for the Chaplet of Divine Mercy.
Daily Scripture: A curated "Verse of the Day" system to provide daily inspiration and reflection.
Common & Special Prayers: From the Apostles' Creed to specific prayers for work, family, and health.

üõ†Ô∏è Technical Architecture
Anollian Mushi prioritized a "Zero-Dependency" philosophy to ensure the app remains fast and future-proof.
Front-End: Semantic HTML5 for SEO and accessibility (Screen reader friendly).
Styling: Advanced CSS3 utilizing Custom Properties (CSS Variables) for the real-time theming engine.
Scripting: Pure Vanilla JavaScript (ES6+). No heavy frameworks like React or Vue were used, ensuring the entire app stays under a few hundred kilobytes.
